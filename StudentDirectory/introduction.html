The Art Portfolio

STILL LIFE STUDY

In Still Life Study, students are required to draw and shade or paint a group of objects which could be man-made or natural.

It may include things such as cut flowers, fruits, vegetables, a growing plant, as well as domestic or other artificial objects of daily use. The discipline develops and sharpens observation as well as drawing skills, gradually enabling the student to depict the group of objects in realistic form, proportions and perspective, as well as to capture the light and shade, the surface texture and the material qualities of the objects.

The students’ eyes are trained to ‘see’ the beauty in ordinary objects and capture it on paper in their artwork. They develop their visual faculty to estimate proportions, sizes and appearances accurately, learning how to use different techniques and mediums of shading and painting.

Above all, the students learn to capture the soul of a composition, infusing their work with inspiration and beauty, by developing a command over their medium of choice and capturing an artistic vision in their art.

APPLIED ART

Applied Art is an introduction to graphic design. Students learn to design book covers, posters, invitation and greeting cards, repeat patterns and logos. The art of Calligraphy and Typography are important components of design, and hence, an introduction to both of these is an integral part of the content.

The emphasis is on developing a sound understanding of composition, visual communication and presentation skills. The young designers are made to understand the role of art in enabling better communication of an idea, information or a product. It is a broad subject area that combines awareness of consumer needs and tastes with the ability to catch their attention and achieve a defined purpose.


PROJECTS

The ICSE and ISC syllabi allows the students to engage amply with various crafts of their choice showcasing their creativity.

In Classes IX-X, the students are required to submit 4 projects in all. They can be pottery, clay modelling, dyeing or printing on textiles, mosaic, embroidery, collage, printing with wood or lino blocks, creative photography, stop-motion or cell animation and many more.

In Classes XI-XII, on the other hand, the students have to pick one craft of their choice and do an individual project of a considerable skill and complexity level. Students have undertaken various kinds of mediums and techniques over the years, like sculpture, mosaic and painted murals, print making from linoleum blocks, silk-screen printing, stencil printing on textiles, pottery with terracotta and ceramic clay, tie-and-dye applique on canvas, installation art etc.

Some of the beautiful projects made by the art students adorn the walls of the school, transforming the space into a more aesthetic and artistically motivating environment.
